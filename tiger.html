National Animal of India

The Royal Bengal Tiger

Panthera tigris tigris

Declared National Animal: 1973

Scientific Name: Panthera tigris

Habitat: Forests, mangroves, grasslands

Conservation Status: Endangered

About the Royal Bengal Tiger:

The Bengal Tiger was adopted as India's national animal in 1973, replacing the lion. It symbolizes strength, agility, and grace. The tiger is at the apex of the food chain and plays a vital role in maintaining the health of the ecosystem.

India is home to over 70% of the world's tiger population, with significant populations found in tiger reserves like Corbett, Bandipur, and Sundarbans.
