National Bird of India

The Indian Peacock

Pavo cristatus

Declared National Bird: 1963

Scientific Name: Pavo cristatus

Habitat: Forests, cultivated lands, near human habitations

Conservation Status: Least Concern

About the Indian Peacock:

The Indian Peacock was declared the national bird of India in 1963 because of its rich religious and legendary involvement in Indian traditions.

The male peacock is known for its extravagant tail feathers which it displays during courtship. In Hindu mythology, the peacock is associated with Saraswati, the goddess of wisdom and learning.

It is fully protected under the Indian Wildlife Protection Act, 1972.
