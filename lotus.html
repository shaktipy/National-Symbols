National Flower of India

The Sacred Lotus

Nelumbo nucifera

Declared National Flower: 1950

Scientific Name: Nelumbo nucifera

Habitat: Freshwater ponds, lakes, and shallow waters

Blooming Season: June to September

About the Lotus Flower:

The Lotus holds a sacred place in Indian culture and is the national flower of India. It symbolizes purity, beauty, majesty, grace, fertility, wealth, knowledge, and enlightenment.

In Indian religions, the lotus is associated with several deities. Lord Vishnu is often described as the "Lotus-Eyed One" and Goddess Lakshmi sits on a fully bloomed lotus flower.

The lotus is unique as it grows in muddy water but rises above the surface to bloom with remarkable beauty, representing how one can rise above worldly attachments.
