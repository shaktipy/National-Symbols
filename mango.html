National Fruit of India

The King of Fruits - Mango

Mangifera indica

Declared National Fruit: 1950

Scientific Name: Mangifera indica

Season: March to July

Major Producing States: Uttar Pradesh, Andhra Pradesh, Karnataka

About the Mango:

Mango, known as the "King of Fruits," has been cultivated in India for over 4,000 years. With over 100 varieties, it's deeply embedded in Indian culture, cuisine, and traditions.

India is the world's largest producer of mangoes, contributing about 50% of the world's total production. Famous varieties include Alphonso, Dasheri, Langra, and Chausa.

In Indian mythology, the mango tree is considered sacred and is often associated with love and fertility. Mango leaves are used in religious ceremonies and decorations during festivals.
